§ 1 — now

Head of Product & Data, iQpay

iQpay issues item-restricted benefit cards for 300+ health-plan and social-support programs, accepted at 60,000+ retail locations across 31+ states. At the register, a system decides product by product what each card can buy. I run product, engineering, and data for that system, reporting to the CEO, and I build the parts that cannot be allowed to be wrong: the classification pipeline, the eval gates, and the billing engine every program is invoiced on.

The pipeline is a CatBoost screener (picked in a 17-model bake-off) that routes uncertain products to a three-model LLM jury, with eval suites and a dry-run diff in front of the live card network. Nothing ships on vibes.

§ 3 — side work

begriff

Frege's Begriffsschrift turned informal language into checkable formalism in 1879. begriff does a toy version of that to protocol specs: normative clauses from the I2C specification in, SystemVerilog assertions out, and nothing trusted on the way: a hand-written golden rulebook with a held-out test split, a TF-IDF baseline the LLM has to beat, a three-model jury, a calibrated judge, a simulation smoke where bad traces must trip the assertions, and a release gate a human has to approve.
