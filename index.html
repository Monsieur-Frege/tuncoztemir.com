Now / iQpay

I run product, engineering, and data. I build the parts that cannot be wrong.

iQpay issues item-restricted benefit cards for 300+ health-plan and social-support programs, accepted at 60,000+ retail locations across 31+ states. At the register, a system decides product by product what each card can buy. I own that system, reporting to the CEO, and I built its classification pipeline, its eval gates, and the billing engine every program is invoiced on.

A CatBoost screener, chosen in a 17-model bake-off, routes uncertain products to a three-model LLM jury. Eval suites and a dry-run diff gate every push to the live card network. Nothing ships on vibes.

What I build

Compliance you can trust with money.

The payment network supports only blocklists. I engineered them into an allowlist verified item by item, which made fresh meat purchasable on our cards for the first time. The same discipline produced a fresh-produce ruleset ratified by the Oregon Health Authority, now live as a 124,571-item catalog serving Oregon Medicaid members directly.

The data platform underneath.

Nielsen and major-retailer feeds, Dockerized scrapers, and in-store field scans I ran myself, unified into 7M+ product records. Shipped as production services on Azure Container Apps with CI, circuit breakers, and encrypted payloads. A 215-test suite and three-way reconciles run on schedule, so regressions surface before members do.

Forensics across boundaries.

I root-cause declines that span our platform, the payment processor, and retailers, undocumented decline taxonomies and shared-account defects included. One integrity sweep audited 28,102 cards and stopped at-risk funding before it left the platform.
